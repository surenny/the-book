Case 1Operator Substitution (sum → product, + → ×)formalrx_5236

easy misaligned Operator Substitution · Semantic
›
Informal / Formal
INFORMAL: Given a finite set s, the sum of f i + g i over i in s equals
          the sum of f i over i in s plus the sum of g i over i in s.

FORMAL:   theorem finprod_mem_mul_distrib :
            ∏ i in s, f i * g i = (∏ i in s, f i) * ∏ i in s, g i
          -- aggregator sum→prod, inner op +→*
Diagnosis
verdict
Misaligned
category
Operator Substitution (Semantic)
segment
the whole RHS expression with prod/*
corrected

Insight. Structure intact but both the aggregator (sum→product) and the inner operator (+→*) are swapped. A frontier LLM should one-shot this. Typical "easy" sample.

Case 5Conclusion Quantifier Inversion — ∃→∀formalrx_2537

hard misaligned Quantifier Inversion · Severe
›
Informal / Formal
INFORMAL: ...Is it NECESSARY that sum_digits(ab) = sum_digits(a) * sum_digits(b)?
          Prove that the answer is: No

FORMAL:   theorem omni_theorem_4388 {a b : ℕ} (ha : isPedestrian a)
          (hb : isPedestrian b) (hc : isPedestrian (a * b)) :
            (digits 10 (a*b)).sum ≠ (digits 10 a).sum * (digits 10 b).sum
Diagnosis
verdict
Misaligned
category
Conclusion Quantifier Inversion (Semantic, severe)
segment
the universal conclusion (asserting ≠ for all pedestrian a, b)
corrected
∃ a b : ℕ, isPedestrian a ∧ isPedestrian b ∧ isPedestrian (a*b) ∧ (digits 10 (a*b)).sum ≠ (digits 10 a).sum * (digits 10 b).sum
Insight. "Answer is No" to "is it necessary?" means a counterexample exists, not "the equation fails for all inputs". The formal as written is actually a false proposition (take a=b=1: both sides equal 1). Pattern — "Find / Prove that the answer is X" — is a high-frequency trap throughout the dataset.

FORMAL:   Set.ncard {x : ℕ × ℕ | ... x.1 - x.2 < sqrt (5 + 4 * sqrt (4*m + 1))}

Diagnosis
verdict
Misaligned

segment

coerce both sides to ℝ and use Real.sqrt. Also: x.1 - x.2 uses truncating Nat subtraction; the comparison should be over integers.
Insight. Argument types make Lean elaborate sqrt as Nat.sqrt (integer floor) rather than Real.sqrt. The values differ enormously (e.g. Nat.sqrt 12 = 3 vs Real.sqrt 12 ≈ 3.46). You cannot see this from informal text alone — you must mentally elaborate the formal statement. This is where strong LLMs commonly fail.

Practical implications for prompt design

01

Always include the header field

Case 7 cannot be diagnosed without knowing the inferred types from the imports/variables block — informal + formal alone is not enough.

02

Add a paraphrase-aware aligned check

Prompt the model to enumerate Mathlib idioms ("X is unique" ⇔ "map injective", "X is the same" ⇔ "∀ point equal"). Without this, aligned samples are systematically rejected.

03

Handle answer-type questions separately

"Prove that the answer is …" / "Find the value …" carry an implicit existential quantifier (counterexample for "No", witness for "Yes"). Naive ∀ translations are wrong.

04

Bidirectional self-check at verdict stage

Have the model argue both aligned and misaligned, then pick the better-supported side. Cuts false positives on paraphrased aligned cases.

05

Constant- and predicate-level token diff

Many errors (Cases 3, 4) are one-token substitutions. A pass that enumerates predicates, constants, quantifiers, operators and verifies each pairwise is cheap and high-yield.

Open questions for development
